THE FRAMING

FUND, not as a portfolio project. Every decision is rated against the
fund-seed bar (provable edge, capital-grade infra, LP-credible
reporting), not the student/intern bar.

Tier 1 is the next thing that has to happen. It is methodology
validation: re-run the existing factor research stack on a defensible
substrate to find out whether ANY signal in this universe survives a
deflation-aware, cost-honest gauntlet. Pass or fail decides whether
Tier 2 is justified.


THE TIER 1 BINARY GATE  (write it on a wall)
--------------------------------------------

By end of month 9-10, on a point-in-time S&P 500 universe, with a
realistic cost model, deflated for the full search:

  PASS:  at least one signal (single or combined) has DSR > 0.95 OOS
         net of costs, with bootstrap Sharpe CI excluding zero,
         replicated across two non-overlapping OOS sub-windows.

  FAIL:  no survivor → do not proceed to Tier 2. Reassess via the
         failure-path decision matrix.

The gate is binary. Not "I tried hard." Not "the methodology is good."
Did a signal survive — yes or no.

[IN PROGRESS]  PHASE 3 — RISK-MODEL RESIDUALIZATION  (pipeline runs end-to-end; methodology gate failing)
============================================================================

Why
---
Every IC and Sharpe in the legacy factor study is contaminated by
FF5 + momentum exposure. The equal-weight baseline crushes everything
because the universe's beta is the dominant return source.
Residualizing strips that out so we measure ALPHA, not BETA.

Required validation before Phase 4 trusts it
--------------------------------------------
  - FF5 factors built from the universe must have correlation > 0.85
    against Ken French's published factor returns over the overlap
    period (MKT, SMB, HML, RMW, CMA, plus UMD).
  - Below 0.85 → factor construction is wrong; do NOT proceed.
  - Spot-test: residualizing the size factor against itself must drive
    its SMB-loading to ≈ 0.

Math depth note: this phase requires re-deriving Fama-MacBeth
regressions by hand. That is not overhead — it is the parallel skill
work happening anyway.
